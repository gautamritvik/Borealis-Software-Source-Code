# The Borealis Manifesto

*Version 1.0 — Ritvik Gautam, 2026*

---

## What Is Borealis?

Borealis is a free and open-source software initiative built around a single belief: **software should be designed for people, not around infrastructure.**

Borealis is not a company. It is not a product. It is a movement — a collection of projects unified by a philosophy. Every tool under the Borealis name exists because something was harder than it needed to be, and we decided to fix it.

---

## What Is MFOSS?

MFOSS stands for **Modern Free and Open Source Software.**

The free software movement gave the world something extraordinary. It proved that software built in the open, by people who believe in sharing, could be more powerful, more trustworthy, and more durable than anything built behind closed doors. We owe everything to that foundation.

But the world has changed. AI is no longer a research topic — it is a daily tool. The way people write, think, and build has shifted. And most open-source software has not kept up.

MFOSS is the next step. It takes the core values of free software — freedom, transparency, community, and openness — and applies them to the problems of today. Not software from the 1990s reimagined. Software built for now, with the tools that exist now, for the people who are building now.

---

## What We Believe

**1. AI should be a right, not a premium feature.**
Every person who writes code or creates documents deserves access to intelligent tools — not locked behind a subscription, not buried in a third-party API, not requiring a computer science degree to configure. AI belongs in the hands of everyone.

**2. Simplicity is a design principle, not a limitation.**
Complexity is easy. Simplicity is hard. The best software does one thing clearly and does it well. We would rather ship something small that works than something large that overwhelms.

**3. Open source is non-negotiable.**
Every line of code Borealis produces is open. Not open-core. Not "community edition." Fully open, forever, under licenses that protect that openness. This is not a business strategy. It is a commitment.

**4. The tools you use shape the way you think.**
A programming language is not just syntax — it is a worldview. A word processor is not just an editor — it is a thinking environment. We take that seriously. We build tools that expand what feels possible, not tools that constrain you to what already exists.

**5. Software should be installable by a student, not just a sysadmin.**
If your project requires a PhD to set up, it will only ever be used by people with PhDs. We build for the person who is learning, the person who is building their first thing, the person who just wants it to work.

## The Projects

### Curl
Curl is a programming language where AI is native syntax — not a library, not an import, not an afterthought. `pcAI.ask{}` is as fundamental to Curl as `print` is to Python. Curl exists because the distance between "I have an idea" and "I have a working program" should be as short as possible.

Curl ships with Curl-Bot, a fine-tuned model trained on Curl's own syntax, available for free via Hugging Face. You do not need an OpenAI account. You do not need a credit card. You need one environment variable and you are building.

### Textitor
Textitor is an AI-integrated word processor built for the way people actually write — not the way enterprise software assumes they write. It is lightweight, open-source, and designed around the idea that your editor should help you think, not just format your thoughts after the fact.

Textitor is under active development. More soon.

## What Borealis Is Not

Borealis is not trying to replace Linux, replace Python, or replace Microsoft Office.

Borealis is not a startup. There is no Series A coming. There is no acquisition exit. There is no roadmap driven by what advertisers want.

Borealis is not finished. These are early days. The code is imperfect, the documentation is incomplete, and there is more to build than has been built. That is honest. We would rather be honest than impressive.

---

## Who Can Contribute

Anyone.

If you believe software should be free and open, if you have ever been frustrated by how hard it is to do something simple, if you want to build tools that treat users as intelligent adults — you belong here.

You do not need to be a senior engineer. You do not need prior open-source experience. You need curiosity, a willingness to learn, and a belief that the things we build together are worth building.

---

## The Name

Borealis. The northern lights — a natural phenomenon that is rare, vivid, and impossible to own. It appears without warning and lights up everything around it.

That is what we are trying to build. Software that illuminates. Software that belongs to everyone.
